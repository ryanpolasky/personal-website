I.About

Quietly building software that works, even when no-one's watching.

I'm a fourth-year Computer Science student at the University of Texas at Dallas, graduating in May 2026. I specialize in solving the unglamorous problems that make engineering teams ten times faster — intelligent log analysis, bulletproof CI/CD pipelines, test automation that actually works, and AI-powered query generation that doesn't fall over the moment a user phrases a question oddly.

At Apple, I built LLM-powered tooling for the Camera and Photos team, automated the triage of thousands of flaky test failures with smart categorization, and designed cloud workflows that ship fast without breaking anything. I balance velocity with reliability, security with usability, and prioritize crystal-clear communication. Half of engineering, in my view, is writing things down well.

Outside of work I produce lo-fi music as plasky (published since 2023), maintain a public knowledge base of my CS curriculum, and keep a weather eye on the things that go wrong in production at 3am — because that's where the real lessons live.
